Our Story

At the time of its release, the W1 was Japan's largest-displacement motorcycle, and boasted top-level horsepower. Designed to be the fastest, most powerful machines of their day, Kawasaki's motorcycles are now better known for a beauty that never goes out of style, and the rich character of their engineering.

To this day, Japan's original big bikes continue to command a deep respect. To ride a Kawasaki is to feel the pride of being connected to such illustrious roots.

"Let the Good Times Roll" — More than a slogan, it's a promise of the thrill and freedom that comes with every Kawasaki.

From the groundbreaking W1 to the supercharged Ninja H2, Kawasaki has consistently pushed the boundaries of what's possible on two wheels. Every motorcycle we build carries the DNA of our racing heritage and our unwavering commitment to innovation.

Milestones

1966

First Motorcycle - W1

Kawasaki produces its first motorcycle - the 624cc W1. Japan's largest-displacement motorcycle of its time.

1972

Z1 900 - The King

The legendary Z1 revolutionizes the industry with a 903cc engine, 82hp, and top speed of 130mph.

1984

First Ninja - GPZ900R

The Ninja name is born. The GPZ900R becomes the world's first 16-valve, liquid-cooled production motorcycle.

1990

ZZ-R1100

World's fastest production motorcycle with 147hp and top speed of 175mph.

2003

ZX-10R

Ninja ZX-10R redefines the liter-class superbike segment.

2015

Ninja H2

Supercharged hypersport masterpiece with 310hp - engineering at its peak.

2024

Electric Future

Kawasaki announces first electric motorcycles, beginning a new era.
